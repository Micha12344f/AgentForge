# Hedge Edge — Prop Firm Hedging Explained

> **Important**: Hedge Edge is a tool for **traders** (our customers) — NOT for prop firms.
> The trader uses Hedge Edge to protect their own capital while attempting prop firm challenges.
> Prop firms are the companies that issue the challenges; they are NOT Hedge Edge users.

A beginner-friendly guide to understanding how hedging protects **your** capital when trading prop firm challenges.

---

## The Problem

Prop firm challenges have strict rules:
- You must hit a profit target (usually 8-10%)
- You must not exceed a maximum drawdown (usually 5-10%)
- If you fail, you lose your challenge fee ($100-500+)

**The reality: 85-90% of prop firm challenges fail.** Most traders blow their accounts before reaching the target.

---

## The Solution: Hedging

Hedging means opening an **opposite trade** on a separate account to offset your risk.

### Example:
1. You BUY 1 lot of EUR/USD on your **prop firm** account
2. Hedge Edge automatically SELLS 1 lot of EUR/USD on your **personal broker** account
3. Now you're market-neutral:
   - If EUR/USD goes UP → prop account profits, personal account loses (equal amounts)
   - If EUR/USD goes DOWN → prop account loses, personal account profits (equal amounts)

### Why is this useful?

The key insight: **you're playing a different game on each account**.

- **Prop firm account**: You need to hit a profit target. If you succeed, you get a funded account worth $10K-200K+
- **Personal broker account**: This is your safety net. Losses here are offset by wins on the prop side

Over multiple trades, you're essentially "transferring" risk from the prop firm challenge to your personal account — where you control the rules.

## Key Concepts

### Hedge Ratio
The ratio between your prop firm position and your hedge position. A 1:1 hedge ratio means equal lot sizes on both sides (full hedge). Some traders use partial hedges (e.g., 0.5:1) to keep some directional exposure.

### Spread Cost
Each trade has a spread (the difference between buy and sell price). When hedging, you pay the spread on **both** accounts. This is the cost of protection. Using brokers with tight spreads (like Vantage or BlackBull) minimizes this cost.

### Capital Required
You need enough capital in your personal broker account to support the hedge positions. The amount depends on:
- Size of your prop firm challenges
- Instruments traded
- Leverage offered by your broker

### Prop Firm Rules
**Important**: Some prop firms have rules about specific trading strategies. Always read your prop firm's terms carefully. Hedging on a **separate personal account** is generally permitted, but rules vary.

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Challenge Success
- You pass the prop firm challenge by hitting the profit target
- Your personal hedge account may show a net loss equal to the prop profits minus spreads
- **Result**: You now have a funded account worth $25K-200K+ for the cost of spreads and challenge fee

### Scenario 2: Challenge Failure (With Hedge)
- Your prop firm account hits maximum drawdown
- Your personal hedge account shows equivalent profits
- **Result**: Your capital is preserved — the personal account gains offset the challenge fee

### Scenario 3: No Hedge (Traditional Approach)
- Your prop firm account hits maximum drawdown
- You lose the challenge fee ($100-500+)
- **Result**: Money gone, start over
