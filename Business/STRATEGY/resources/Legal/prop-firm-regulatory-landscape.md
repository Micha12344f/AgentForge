# Prop Firm Regulatory Landscape — By Jurisdiction
> Intelligence on the regulatory status of proprietary trading firms and hedging legality
> Critical for Hedge Edge's market risk assessment

---

## Overview

Proprietary trading firms ("prop firms") operate in a regulatory grey area in most jurisdictions. This impacts Hedge Edge because:
1. If prop firms face regulatory crackdowns, our addressable market shrinks
2. If hedging is banned by regulators (not just prop firms), our product may face restrictions
3. Understanding the regulatory landscape helps us advise users and position our marketing

---

## United Kingdom

### Regulatory Status of Prop Firms
- **FCA view**: Most prop firms are NOT regulated by the FCA
- **Reason**: Prop firms provide funded accounts (simulated or real) — they are not typically categorised as investment firms, fund managers, or brokers
- **Key distinction**: If the trader is trading the firm's capital (not their own), it may fall outside MiFID II investment services
- **Risk**: The FCA could reclassify prop firms as requiring authorisation — this is an ongoing uncertainty

### Hedging Legality
- **Legal**: Hedging is a legitimate trading strategy in the UK
- **No restriction**: There is no FCA rule prohibiting hedging across accounts
- **Prop firm rules**: Individual prop firms may restrict hedging in their terms (contractual, not regulatory)

### Impact on Hedge Edge
- **LOW risk**: Our software is a trade management tool, not a regulated product
- **MEDIUM risk**: If prop firms are regulated and ban hedging, demand decreases
- **Action**: Monitor FCA consultations on prop firm regulation

## European Union

### Regulatory Status of Prop Firms
- **ESMA view**: Varies by member state; no harmonised EU approach
- **MiFID II**: Some prop firms may fall under investment firm regulation if they facilitate client trading
- **Key countries**:
  - **Cyprus (CySEC)**: Several prop firms operate from Cyprus; CySEC has started scrutinising
  - **Netherlands (AFM)**: Has issued warnings about unregulated prop firms
  - **Germany (BaFin)**: Increasingly vigilant on fintech classification
  - **France (AMF)**: Generally less active on prop firm regulation

### Hedging Legality
- **Legal**: Hedging is permitted across the EU
- **Note**: ESMA's leverage restrictions (2018) affected retail traders but not hedging as a strategy
- **Prop firm rules**: Same as UK — contractual restrictions, not regulatory

### GDPR (EU)
- **Full EU GDPR applies** if serving EU customers
- **Cross-border**: EU GDPR is stricter on some points than UK GDPR (e.g., lead supervisory authority, one-stop-shop mechanism)
- **Action**: If expanding to EU, appoint an EU representative (Article 27)

## United States

### Regulatory Status of Prop Firms
- **SEC/CFTC**: Prop firms dealing in futures may fall under CFTC oversight
- **Key risk**: CFTC has taken action against some firms offering funded trading accounts
- **NFA**: Prop firms dealing in forex may need NFA registration
- **Reality**: Many US-facing prop firms are offshore (to avoid these requirements)

### Hedging Legality
- **Complex**: FIFO (First In, First Out) rule for US forex accounts prohibits same-pair hedging within a single account
- **Cross-account**: Hedging across different brokers is technically possible but frowned upon
- **Prop firm angle**: Most prop firms serving US clients are offshore, so US regulations apply differently

### Impact on Hedge Edge
- **Do NOT market to US users initially** — regulatory complexity is too high
- **Future**: If entering US market, need legal counsel on CFTC/NFA compliance

## Australia

### Regulatory Status of Prop Firms
- **ASIC**: Has increased scrutiny of prop firms operating in/from Australia
- **2023-2024**: Several prop-firm-style products flagged by ASIC
- **Key concern**: Consumer protection around failed challenges and fee structures

### Hedging Legality
- **Legal**: No restrictions on hedging in Australia
- **Vantage partnership**: Vantage is ASIC-regulated — IB compliance straightforward

## Southeast Asia (SEA)

### Key Markets
- **Malaysia**: Growing prop firm user base; BNM has limited oversight of prop firms
- **Indonesia**: OJK has flagged some prop firms as potential investment scams
- **Philippines**: SEC Philippines has warned about unlicensed trading platforms
- **Thailand**: SEC Thailand has restricted some online trading products

### Hedging Legality
- **Generally legal**: No specific restrictions on hedging in most SEA jurisdictions
- **Risk**: Increasing regulatory attention on online trading products

### Impact on Hedge Edge
- **OPPORTUNITY**: Large, underserved market
- **RISK**: Regulatory uncertainty; potential for sudden bans
- **Action**: Localise disclaimers; don't promise returns; emphasise educational content

## Middle East & North Africa (MENA)

### Key Markets
- **UAE (DFSA/SCA)**: Dubai is a hub for prop firms; relatively permissive
- **Saudi Arabia (CMA)**: Restricts certain online trading products
- **Egypt**: CMA Egypt has flagged forex trading products

### Hedging Legality
- **Generally legal**: Hedging is not specifically restricted
- **Islamic finance**: Consider Sharia-compliant hedging options (swap-free accounts)

### Impact on Hedge Edge
- **OPPORTUNITY**: UAE and Gulf states are high-value markets
- **Action**: Consider swap-free account support; localise Arabic content

3. **Hedging banned by prop firms industry-wide**: Impact = CRITICAL (existential threat; pivot to risk management positioning)

5. **US market opens up**: Impact = POSITIVE (massive TAM expansion; requires CFTC compliance)
