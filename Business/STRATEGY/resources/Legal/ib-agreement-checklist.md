# IB Agreement Review Checklist — Hedge Edge Ltd
> Checklist for reviewing Introducing Broker agreements before signing
> Applicable to: Vantage, BlackBull, and future broker partners

---

## Pre-Signing Due Diligence

### 1. Broker Regulatory Status

- [ ] Broker is regulated by a reputable authority (FCA, ASIC, CySEC, FSA)
- [ ] Check the regulator's public register for the broker's licence
- [ ] No regulatory actions, fines, or sanctions in the last 3 years
- [ ] Broker has adequate professional indemnity insurance
- [ ] Broker is solvent (check latest accounts if available)

### 2. Agreement Structure

- [ ] Agreement clearly defines the IB relationship (not an employee or agent)
- [ ] Territory/scope is defined (global or specific regions)
- [ ] Exclusivity terms reviewed (non-exclusive preferred)
- [ ] Term/duration specified (with reasonable termination notice)
- [ ] Auto-renewal terms clear and acceptable

### 3. Commission Structure

- [ ] Commission model clearly defined (per-lot, spread share, CPA, hybrid)
- [ ] Commission rates specified and competitive
- [ ] Payment frequency stated (monthly, bi-weekly)
- [ ] Minimum payout threshold reasonable
- [ ] Currency of payment specified
- [ ] Commission on sub-IBs (if applicable) defined
- [ ] Trailing commissions (lifetime of client) vs. time-limited

### 4. Client Ownership & Data

- [ ] Clarity on who "owns" the client relationship
- [ ] What client data does the broker share with the IB?
- [ ] GDPR implications of receiving client data
- [ ] How are referred clients tracked (link, code, manual)?
- [ ] What happens to trailing commissions if agreement terminates?
- [ ] Client data handling in line with UK GDPR

### 5. Obligations & Restrictions

- [ ] Review what marketing activities are permitted
- [ ] Check for mandatory disclaimer requirements
- [ ] Review prohibited activities (e.g., executing trades for clients)
- [ ] FCA financial promotions compliance for any IB-branded materials
- [ ] Check if broker needs to approve marketing materials
- [ ] Review confidentiality obligations

### 6. Liability & Indemnification

- [ ] Review indemnification clauses (is the IB indemnifying the broker?)
- [ ] Cap on IB liability (should not exceed commissions earned)
- [ ] Broker indemnifies IB for broker's own negligence/regulatory failures
- [ ] Force majeure provisions
- [ ] Limitation of liability is mutual

### 7. Termination

- [ ] Reasonable notice period for termination (30-90 days)
- [ ] Both parties can terminate for cause
- [ ] Treatment of accrued but unpaid commissions on termination
- [ ] Post-termination obligations (return of materials, confidentiality)
- [ ] No unreasonable clawback provisions

### 8. Dispute Resolution

- [ ] Governing law specified (prefer England & Wales for Hedge Edge)
- [ ] Dispute resolution mechanism (mediation before litigation preferred)
- [ ] Jurisdiction for court proceedings

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **Unlimited IB liability** — Should always be capped
2. **Non-compete clauses** — Prevent you from working with other brokers
3. **Exclusive territory** without adequate consideration
4. **Clawback provisions** — Broker recovers commissions if clients withdraw
5. **Unreasonable termination** — Broker can terminate without notice
6. **Commission forfeiture** — Unpaid commissions lost on termination
7. **Mandatory volume quotas** — Minimum referral requirements
8. **Unilateral rate changes** — Broker can change commission rates without notice
