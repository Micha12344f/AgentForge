Your diagnosis is basically correct. The current wedge is clever, but it sits on top of fragile economics, weak moats, contract risk, and counterparties that may tighten rules the moment your product works too well. The broader research points in the same direction: the MT4, MT5, and cTrader ecosystem is much larger than prop firms, generic trade copier demand already exists well beyond prop use cases, and the durable value is in execution control, risk management, analytics, workflow lock-in, and distribution, not in helping users exploit an unstable niche.

Best Direction

If I had to pick one path, it would be this:

Build the trader operating system for serious MT4, MT5, and cTrader users.
Expand that into broker white-label infrastructure and B2B2C distribution.
Add analytics and telemetry so the moat becomes data, not just features.
Serve creators, educators, and signal businesses as a second strong customer segment.
Keep prop as a wedge or customer segment, not as the company thesis.
