

1. Market Intelligence & Discovery: The "Pain-Point Safari"


Since you cannot run sales calls yet, you must run pure discovery.

The Rep: Schedule 2–3 calls per week with operators, founders, or domain experts in industries you find interesting.
The Goal: Do not pitch anything. Ask them to walk you through their day and specifically look for the "swivel-chair problems"—where they export to Excel, manually reconcile data, or use outdated legacy software.
The Output: Log at least 1 observation daily in your workspace using the format: "I noticed [Process] is painful/broken/manual for [Role] in [Industry] context."


2. Strategic Judgment: Practice Killing Ideas Analytically


Your job will be to say "no" to weak ideas with evidence. You can practice this right now.

The Rep: Pull an existing business model or a random startup idea from the internet every Tuesday and Thursday.
The Goal: Run it through your business-thesis-template.md and force it through a brutal unit economics and moat stress-test.
The Output: Write a theoretical "Kill or Go" memo for that idea. Practice finding the fatal flaw (e.g., "The CAC will eventually exceed LTV because distribution relies entirely on paid search").


3. Financial Literacy: Reverse-Engineer Unit Economics


Financial literacy is best learned by tearing down real machines.

The Rep: Pick 3 successful SaaS or service businesses.
The Goal: Back-of-the-napkin their economics. Estimate their Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Gross Margins, and Payback Periods based on their pricing pages and estimated sales cycles.
The Output: Build a basic cashflow and runway dashboard in Excel or Sheets. Have it completely sterile and ready so that on Day 1 of your actual idea, you just plug in the variables.


4. Operating Discipline: Build the CRM and Pipeline


Nothing important should live only in your head.

The Rep: Set up your operating environment today.
The Goal: Configure a lightweight CRM (HubSpot, Attio, or even an Airtable/Notion board). Build stages for: Lead Identified -> Cold Outreach -> Discovery Scheduled -> Pipeline -> Closed/Lost.
The Output: Create the templates you will need: cold email sequences, discovery call question scripts, and daily metric scoreboards.


5. Start the 14-Day Idea Blitz (From the Orchestrator Playbook)



Adopt the AgentForge sprint cadence to force idea generation:

Days 1–5: Pure observation. Log one broken workflow per day.
Day 6: Review your logged observations. Do any pass basic feasibility criteria? If yes, promote them to "Gate 0".
Day 12: Review the Gate 0 ideas. Run a quick-kill sanity check (are the margins inherently terrible? Is the market too small?).
Day 14: Pick the strongest surviving problem space to enter "Gate 2" deep research, or scrap them all and restart the loop.
